Slow Cooker Pulled Pork

Description

Pulled pork is a classic dish typically made from slow-cooked pork shoulder that is seasoned and cooked until tender, allowing the meat to be easily shredded or "pulled" apart. It's often seasoned with a blend of spices, marinated, and cooked with barbecue sauce or a vinegar-based sauce, depending on regional styles. The dish is commonly served on a bun as a sandwich, paired with coleslaw, or as part of a platter with sides like cornbread and baked beans. The result is a flavorful, juicy meat that melts in your mouth, offering a rich, smoky, and savory experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 (2 pound) pork tenderloin
  • 1 (12 fluid ounce) can or bottle root beer
  • 1 (18 ounce) bottle your favorite barbecue sauce
  • 8 hamburger buns, split and lightly toasted

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Place pork tenderloin in a slow cooker; pour root beer over top.
  3. Cover and cook on Low until pork shreds easily, 6 to 7 hours. Note: the actual length of time may vary according to the individual slow cooker.
  4. Drain well. Stir in barbecue sauce.
  5. Serve on hamburger buns.
  6. Serve and enjoy!
